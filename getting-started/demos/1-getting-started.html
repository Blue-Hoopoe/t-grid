1. Getting started

1.1 Simple one third grid

To create simple one third grid all you need to do is define t-grid, one t-row and insert three t-col t-xxs-4 columns inside them.

Hello! Me and my sibling take 1/3 of this row.
Unless you shrink us!
Then we will automatically adjust. 
<div class="t-grid">
    <div class="t-row">
        <div class="t-col t-xxs-4">
            Hello! Me and my sibling take 1/3 of this row.
        </div>
        <div class="t-col t-xxs-4">
            Unless you shrink us!
            </div>
        <div class="t-col t-xxs-4">
            Then we will automatically adjust.
        </div>
    </div>
</div>

1.2 Auto columns sizes

In contrast to float-grid, when t-grid is displaying as a table, its columns are being auto sized. You can force auto sizes by adding t-{breakpoint}-auto class or by just ommitting the t-{breakpoint}-{size} classes.

I'm always taking 50% of the space.
And I take as much space as I can.
So do I! 
<div class="t-grid">
    <div class="t-row">
        <div class="t-col t-xxs-6">
            I'm always taking 50% of the space.
        </div>
        <div class="t-col t-xxs-auto">
            And I take as much space as I can.
            </div>
        <div class="t-col">
            So do I!
        </div>
    </div>
</div>

1.3 Different column sizes at different breakpoints

Just like in a float-grids, you can force column size you want. By default, t-grid has twelve columns in a row and five breakpoints, so just use them however you want!

Just resize us!
And see what happen...
...and when happen! 
<div class="t-grid">
    <div class="t-row">
        <div class="t-col t-xs-5 t-md-6">
            Just resize us!
        </div>
        <div class="t-col t-xs-2 t-md-3">
            And see what happen...
            </div>
        <div class="t-col t-xs-5 t-md-3">
            ...and when happen!
        </div>
    </div>
</div>
